Ulam's spiral

The Ulam spiral is a famous method of visualizing the prime numbers. It was discovered by the mathematician Stanis≈Çaw Ulam in 1963 during a boring presentation, when he started writing the natural numbers on a graphed paper in the shape of a spiral. He then marked all the prime numbers and to his surprise he found, that almost all of the prime numbers lay on diagonals.
This dialog draws the Ulam spiral. You can enter the interval of the displayed numbers and rotate and zoom the spiral with the two sliding controls.
