Primitive root of a prime number

A primitive root modulo m is a term from number theory, an area of mathematics.
A primitive root is a special element of the prime residues with the property, that each of the prime residues can be represented as a power of the primitive root.
For example, 6 is a primitive root of the prime residues of 11. The prime residues of 11 are the numbers 1 to 10.

6 is a primitive root, since the powers take each value between 1 and 10 exactly once:
60 mod 11 = 1
61 mod 11 = 6
62 mod 11 = 3
63 mod 11 = 7
64 mod 11 = 9
65 mod 11 = 10
66 mod 11 = 5
67 mod 11 = 8
68 mod 11 = 4
69 mod 11 = 2
